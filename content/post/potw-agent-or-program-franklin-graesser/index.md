So, I'm a researcher over all. No matter with roles I had assumed during my carraer, the researcher mindset was there and now I'm officialy working with it. Given my current attributions, a enourmous part of may daily (or at least weekly) activities is to read papers. I read them for my own reserach, to review them, to elaborate projects, to learn about my research topic or to learn about the most recent breakthrough in the tech world.

Altough some papers are so important that I can even quote parts of them, it's quite common to read some papers and forget or keep them on hold until I read something else and it comes back to my mind (some papers are forever forgotten, but I believe thay can back anytime). When it happens, the taken notes of initial reading(s) are essential to remember and help to use it to buil an argument, proposal, or any relevant content.

That's why I decide to take some of my notes, organize them within a understandable text, include some extra comments and post them as individual post I decide to call Paper Of The Week (POTW), a series that will not be weeakly, since it will driven me insiane, but where I'll try to bring different and interesting insights I got from the papers.

The note below will be the header of all fllowing POTW posts.

{{< callout note >}}
The Paper Of The Week (POTW) series is a series of post where I select a paper I have read recentely (less than 6 months) and share my toughts about it. If possible, I can try to reproduce the paper results. Important to note that usually, this is not a critical review, but rather a insightful analysis of the paper proposal. My filter to select the paper is quite random: classical relevant papers, new breakthroughs, hidden gems are all included. My intention here is not replace a careful reading or analysis, but give my views and toughts on given topic.
{{< /callout >}}
